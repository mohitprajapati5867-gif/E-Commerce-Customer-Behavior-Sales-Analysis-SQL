📊 Project Overview

This project presents an end-to-end SQL-based analysis of an e-commerce dataset aimed at understanding customer behavior, product performance, and overall business growth. The primary objective is to transform raw transactional data into meaningful and actionable insights using structured query language (SQL).

The analysis begins with comprehensive data cleaning and validation, including handling missing values, detecting duplicates, correcting inconsistent data, and validating data types to ensure accuracy and reliability.

Key performance indicators (KPIs) are calculated to evaluate business performance, such as Total Revenue, Total Orders, Total Customers, Average Order Value, and Revenue per Customer. These metrics provide a strong foundation for understanding overall sales efficiency.

The project further explores multiple analytical dimensions:

- Customer Analysis: Segmentation based on demographics (age, gender, country) and behavior patterns
- Product & Category Analysis: Identification of top-performing products and high-revenue categories
- Country-wise Analysis: Revenue contribution and market performance across regions
- Discount Analysis: Impact of discounts on revenue and purchasing behavior
- Behavior Analysis: Insights based on device type, session duration, and referral sources
- Time-Based Analysis: Monthly sales trends, peak and lowest revenue periods, and growth patterns

Advanced SQL techniques such as JOINs, aggregate functions, Common Table Expressions (CTEs), and window functions are used to perform in-depth analysis and derive meaningful insights.

This project demonstrates how SQL can be effectively used in real-world business scenarios to support data-driven decision making and uncover valuable opportunities for growth and optimization.
